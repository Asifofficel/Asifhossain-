Montage Editing:

Focuses on creating a fast-paced sequence of short shots to convey information or evoke emotions.

Cutting on Action:

Edits made at points of physical movement or action for smooth transitions.

Match Cut:

Seamlessly transitions between two shots with similar visual elements for continuity.

Jump Cut:

Creates a jarring effect by cutting between shots of the same subject, often used for artistic expression.

Cross-cutting (Parallel Editing):

Alternates between two or more scenes happening simultaneously to build tension or convey a connection.

Montage Editing:

Involves assembling a series of short shots or images to condense time or convey information quickly.

VFX Editing:

Integrates visual effects into footage to create fantastical or impossible scenarios.

Color Grading:

Enhances or alters the colors in a video to create a specific mood or visual style.

Sound Editing:

Manipulates audio elements to enhance the overall auditory experience, including music, dialogue, and sound effects.

Documentary Editing:

Involves structuring footage to tell a factual story, often combining interviews, voiceovers, and archival material.
